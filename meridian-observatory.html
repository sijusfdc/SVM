Meridian sits on a ridge with no town for forty kilometers in any direction. The nearest streetlight is a memory.

We built the dome in 1994 for research use, and opened the mountain to visitors a decade later once we realized the sky here was too good to keep to ourselves. The same 0.7m reflector that tracks near-Earth asteroids on weeknights spends clear Saturdays pointed wherever our guests want to look.

No shuttle buses, no gift shop lighting. You arrive before dusk, your eyes adjust on the walk up, and by the time the dome opens you're already seeing more stars than most people see in a year.
